Ghatasthapana (Kalash Sthapana) - Navratri

Kalash (Sacred Pot)

A brass, copper, or clay pot filled with holy water, adorned with mango leaves and a coconut, is the central element.

Ghatasthapana Muhurat

06:13 AM to 10:21 AM (Check for local timings)

Abhijit Muhurat: 12:00 PM to 12:50 PM (Check for local timings)

Puja Samagri (Items Required)

  • Kalash (Sacred Pot)
  • Mango Leaves
  • Coconut (wrapped in a red cloth)
  • Unpeeled Barley (Jau)
  • Soil
  • Water
  • Incense Sticks
  • Flowers
  • Fruits
  • Sweets
  • Mouli (Sacred thread)
  • Roli (Red powder)
  • Rice
  • Turmeric

Significance of Ghatasthapana

Ghatasthapana marks the beginning of the nine-day Navratri festival, symbolizing the invocation of Goddess Shakti. It is believed that performing the ritual at the auspicious muhurat ensures peace and prosperity for the household. [1, 2]
