Introduction

Hiware Bazar is a village in Ahmednagar district, Maharashtra, known for its remarkable transformation into a model village. [10, 7] It's a story of water conservation, community development, and sustainable practices.

History

Historically, Hiware Bazar was a prosperous trading center, located on the edge of Shivaji's territory. It faced challenges like water scarcity and poverty before its transformation.

Transformation

Under the leadership of Popatrao Pawar, the village implemented various initiatives including water conservation projects and the "four bandis" (restrictions on tree felling, free grazing, liquor consumption, and family planning) [5, 8, 12].

Key Figures

Popatrao Pawar, the Sarpanch of Hiware Bazar, played a pivotal role in the village's development. [9, 4]

Notable Practices

The "four bandis" (kurhad bandi, charai bandi, nashabandi, and nasbandi) are key to the village's success [8, 12].
